This crypto trading system approaches short-term BTC prediction markets as a systematic trading problem rather than pure speculation.

The bot continuously ingests market and contextual data, normalizes signals through a unified pipeline, combines multiple detection models into a decision engine, and executes trades through a broker adapter with strict risk management rules.

Instead of relying on a single “magic indicator,” the strategy focuses on:

signal aggregation,
simulation-first testing,
observability through Grafana,
and disciplined execution with predefined position sizing and take-profit logic.

The goal is to build a scalable, testable, and production-grade trading infrastructure where risk control and system reliability matter just as much as prediction accuracy.

This Polymarket weather strategy is based on buying large volumes of extremely low-priced “No” positions (1¢ odds) across weather-event markets.

The trader accepts many small losses in exchange for occasional massive payouts, where a single win can offset hundreds of failed bets.

Despite only a ~27% win rate, the asymmetric risk/reward structure produced ~$13.8K profit and 431% ROI through disciplined bankroll management, high trade volume, and convex payoff exposure.

In simple terms:
small consistent losses + rare outsized wins = long-term profitability.

# Sports Bot

## FIFA World Cup

https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b024d9cd-7cbf-4b1b-9618-ff45e4f9bdbd

This sports trading strategy focuses on buying low-cost positions across all teams early in major tournaments like the FIFA World Cup, then profiting from volatility as market odds shift over time.

Instead of predicting the exact winner, the trader exploits price movement and liquidity inefficiencies by entering positions when probabilities are extremely low and exiting after public sentiment and market momentum increase.

The strategy behaves more like volatility trading than traditional sports betting:
small early entries across many outcomes + selective profit-taking during hype cycles = long-term profitability.

This approach previously generated six-figure profits across both sports and esports markets by systematically trading market inefficiencies rather than relying purely on match prediction accuracy.
